More About Me & What I Do

Hi and thank you for visiting me on my little corner of the internet. I'm currently a Computer Science PhD student and a Graduate Research Assitant at the University of Central Florida in Orlando, FL. My advisor is Dr. Carolina Cruz-Neira.

My goal as a PhD student is to be both a scientist and an artist. As more creatives move into VR as a medium for their art, they will need the technical expertise of VR experts. Their art will be driven in part by the technical questions we ask and the work we publish. My hope is to push both VR as an form of expression and a vehicle for compelling narratives while also pushing the boundaires of what the technology is capable of.

Virtual Reality Locomotion

CAVE Locomotion

Project Status: Lit review and implementation
A group of civil engineers at UCF are interested in understanding what modes of VR locomotion will best accomodate for pedestrian behavior in a multi-user VR driving and pedestrian simulator. Alongside another member of the VARLab, we are currently refining implementations of Walking-in-Place, Skateboarding-in-Place, and Omni-Directional Treadmill for use within a CAVE with 3 walls and floor projections.


Redirected Walking vs. Omni-Directional Treadmills: An Evaluation of Presence

Project Status: Submitted to IEEEVR23, waiting for decision.
It remains unknown which of these two solutions, Redirected Walking and Omni-Directional Treadmills, is overall preferred, and how they impact the sense of presence. In order to measure this effect, we conducted a counterbalanced within-subjects user study with 20 participants. We measured presence between the two conditions and gathered quantitative data to measure performance. Our results show both solutions elicit similar amounts of presence, RDW is perceived as more natural between the two and is the preferred choice of the participants.
