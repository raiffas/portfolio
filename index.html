More About Me & What I Do

Hi and thank you for visiting me on my little corner of the internet. I'm currently a Computer Science PhD student and a Graduate Research Assitant at the University of Central Florida in Orlando, FL. My advisor is Dr. Carolina Cruz-Neira.

My goal as a PhD student is to be both a scientist and an artist. As more creatives move into VR as a medium for their art, they will need the technical expertise of VR experts. Their art will be driven in part by the technical questions we ask and the work we publish. My hope is to push both VR as an form of expression and a vehicle for compelling narratives while also pushing the boundaires of what the technology is capable of.

Redirected Walking vs. Omni-Directional Treadmills: An Evaluation of Presence

Project Status: Pre-submission for Virtual Reality (Springer).
It remains unknown which of these two solutions, Redirected Walking and Omni-Directional Treadmills, is overall preferred, and how they impact the sense of presence. In order to measure this effect, we conducted a counterbalanced within-subjects user study with 20 participants. We measured presence between the two conditions and gathered quantitative data to measure performance. Our results show both solutions elicit similar amounts of presence, RDW is perceived as more natural between the two and is the preferred choice of the participants.

Simulator Sickness

Evaluating Methods for the Reduction of Cybersickness in Virtual Reality

Project Status: Conducting user studies
French company Seenetic VR has developed a solution for alleviating simulator sickness. This product can be added to a headset and provides additional self-motion information in a user's peripheral vision. Seenetic has tasked us with conducting a formal evaluation of their device as a method for reducing simulator sickness. User studies are on-going and we aim to publish findings in 2023. If you are local to the Orlando area and would like to participate, please reach out!

Cinematic Virtual Reality

the white room

Project Status: Production for "the white room"
"the white room" is my first foray into VR filmmaking and will be a mixed reality experience that engages a single viewer within an HMD as well as any spectators or passerbys. The main film features solo dancers who move throughout a virtual white volume. The white represents the void, the space between life and death. The dance is a journey through this space, exploring all the emotions that come with it. From this experience, participants and spectators will be charged to reflect on their own feelings towards mortality while connecting to this universal, human experience. The production of the experience will explore experimental 360 degree video stitching techniques, projection based VR, immersive storytelling, and staging mixed reality experience.
